Tell Me Why

Let me clear out some terminology before proceeding.

  • Cryptocurrency: A medium of exchange, such as the US dollar, but is digital and uses encryption techniques to control the creation of monetary units and to verify the transfer of funds. Commonly referred to through text as Crypto.
  • Blockchain: The technology that enables the existence of cryptocurrency.

Mining is the process of verifying transactions which are then added to the blockchain. Miners compete in a system that almost resembles a lottery. This randomness ensures that no one controls the system. You cannot be denied access to this system, all you need is an Internet connection and a smartphone or a laptop to exchange cryptocurrency. With the inclusion and the freedom of the system comes great responsibility. Users are completely in charge of managing their funds, there is no central place or customer service in case the user loses the password for their wallet.

These are the attributes of blockchains that make them unique:

Revolutionary: Similar to the Internet and the automobile, cryptocurrencies are revolutionary and can change the world. The power structure of the current financial system will be shaken and reshaped for the better.

Immutable: Blockchains are immutable and tamper-evident. While our ancestors said written in stone, our children will say written on the blockchain.

Public: Blockchains are public, flat networks between participants. Peer to peer. No one controls what transactions can take place and all the data is verifiable and auditable by anyone.

Collaborative: Gone are the days when our only options are opaque banking systems. Volunteer participants on the protocol work together to run the network and collaborate to establish and enforce consensus rules.

Open: Anyone can participate on the blockchain. There are no barriers to entry, no borders, and no permission is required. If you have an Internet-connected smartphone, you have all you need to participate.

Censorship-resistant: Crypto is resistant to censorship, interference, seizure, manipulation, or control, which makes it neutral to all participants and all transactions. It is not controlled by governments or banks or corporations. Blockchain is controlled by the people.

At its core, blockchains are neutral platforms. These networks do not, cannot prioritize, or censor any transaction based on anything other than the adherence to the consensus protocols.

A financial world truly controlled by computer code would be brutal and would make it a tyranny rather than liberation from the old worn out financial systems and I believe we will see a variety of blockchain implementations with various degrees of decentralization.
